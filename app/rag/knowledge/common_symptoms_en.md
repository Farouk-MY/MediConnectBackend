# Common Symptoms — English Medical Intake Guide

## Headache / Head Pain

### Key Questions for Headache
- Location: frontal, temporal, occipital, diffuse, one-sided?
- Character: throbbing, pressing, sharp, dull, pulsating?
- Duration: how long does each episode last?
- Triggers: stress, bright lights, certain foods, lack of sleep, dehydration?
- Associated symptoms: nausea, vomiting, visual changes, neck stiffness, fever?
- Severity: 1-10 scale, does it affect daily activities?
- History: migraine history, recent head injury, similar episodes before?
- Red flags: sudden severe onset ("thunderclap"), worst headache ever, fever + stiff neck, confusion, vision changes

### Headache — Typical Follow-up Sequence
1. Where exactly is the pain? (Location)
2. How would you describe the pain? (Character — throbbing, sharp, pressure)
3. When did it start? (Onset)
4. How severe is it on a 1-10 scale? (Severity)
5. Any nausea, vision changes, or sensitivity to light? (Associated symptoms)

## Chest Pain

### Key Questions for Chest Pain
- Location: center, left side, right side, radiating to arm/jaw/back?
- Character: sharp, crushing, burning, squeezing, pressure?
- Duration: constant or intermittent? How long?
- Triggers: physical activity, eating, breathing, stress, lying down?
- Associated: shortness of breath, sweating, nausea, dizziness, palpitations?
- History: heart disease, high blood pressure, smoking, diabetes, family history?
- Red flags: crushing chest pain + sweating + shortness of breath → possible cardiac emergency

### Chest Pain — Typical Follow-up Sequence
1. Where exactly in your chest? Does it spread anywhere? (Location + radiation)
2. What does the pain feel like? (Character)
3. How severe is it? (Severity — URGENT: assess quickly)
4. Any shortness of breath, sweating, or dizziness? (Associated — critical for cardiac screening)

## Abdominal / Stomach Pain

### Key Questions for Abdominal Pain
- Location: upper/lower, left/right, central, diffuse?
- Character: cramping, sharp, burning, dull ache, bloating?
- Duration: acute (hours) or chronic (weeks/months)?
- Relation to meals: before, during, or after eating? Which foods?
- Associated: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, blood in stool, fever?
- Menstrual: for women — relation to menstrual cycle?
- History: previous abdominal surgery, GI conditions, food intolerances?

### Abdominal Pain — Typical Follow-up Sequence
1. Where exactly does it hurt? (Location — helps identify organ system)
2. When did it start? (Duration)
3. Is it related to eating? (Triggers)
4. Any nausea, vomiting, or changes in bowel habits? (Associated symptoms)
5. How severe is the pain? (Severity)

## Back Pain

### Key Questions for Back Pain
- Location: upper back, lower back (lumbar), between shoulders, one side?
- Character: sharp, dull, burning, stiffness, radiating to legs?
- Duration: acute or chronic? First time or recurrent?
- Triggers: lifting, bending, sitting for long periods, specific activity?
- Associated: numbness/tingling in legs, weakness, difficulty walking, bladder issues?
- History: previous back injury, heavy physical work, sedentary lifestyle?
- Red flags: numbness in legs, loss of bladder/bowel control, fever with back pain

### Back Pain — Typical Follow-up Sequence
1. Where exactly in your back? (Location)
2. Does it spread to your legs or arms? (Radiation)
3. What were you doing when it started? (Trigger)
4. How long have you had it? (Duration)
5. Any numbness, tingling, or weakness? (Associated — neurological screening)

## Fever

### Key Questions for Fever
- Temperature: measured? How high?
- Duration: how many days?
- Pattern: constant, intermittent, comes and goes?
- Associated: cough, sore throat, body aches, chills, sweating, rash?
- Recent: travel, sick contacts, new medications?
- History: immune conditions, recent surgery, chronic illness?
- Red flags: fever > 40°C, fever with confusion, fever with severe headache + stiff neck

### Fever — Typical Follow-up Sequence
1. How high is the temperature? Did you measure it? (Severity)
2. How many days have you had the fever? (Duration)
3. Any cough, sore throat, or body aches? (Associated symptoms)
4. Have you been around anyone who is sick? (Exposure)
5. Are you taking any medication for it? (Treatment attempted)

## Cough

### Key Questions for Cough
- Type: dry or productive (with mucus)?
- Duration: days, weeks, months?
- Timing: worse at night, morning, after eating, with exercise?
- Mucus: color (clear, yellow, green, blood), amount?
- Associated: fever, shortness of breath, chest pain, wheezing, sore throat?
- History: smoking, asthma, allergies, recent cold/flu, GERD?
- Red flags: coughing blood, severe shortness of breath, high fever

### Cough — Typical Follow-up Sequence
1. Is the cough dry or do you produce mucus? (Type)
2. How long have you had it? (Duration)
3. Do you have fever or shortness of breath? (Associated — severity screening)
4. Do you smoke or have asthma/allergies? (History)

## Sore Throat

### Key Questions for Sore Throat
- Severity: mild discomfort or severe pain?
- Duration: how many days?
- Swallowing: painful to swallow? Difficulty swallowing?
- Associated: fever, cough, runny nose, ear pain, rash, swollen glands?
- History: recurrent sore throats, tonsil issues?
- Red flags: difficulty breathing, inability to swallow saliva, muffled voice

## Dizziness / Vertigo

### Key Questions for Dizziness
- Type: room spinning (vertigo) or lightheaded/faint feeling?
- Duration: seconds, minutes, hours, constant?
- Triggers: standing up, turning head, specific positions?
- Associated: nausea, hearing changes, ear ringing, vision changes, headache?
- History: ear problems, blood pressure issues, anxiety, medications?
- Red flags: sudden onset with headache, difficulty speaking, weakness on one side

## Joint Pain / Musculoskeletal

### Key Questions for Joint Pain
- Which joints: knees, shoulders, hips, hands, ankles, wrists?
- One joint or multiple joints?
- Duration: acute onset or gradual?
- Character: stiffness (morning), swelling, redness, warmth?
- Triggers: activity, rest, weather changes, injury?
- Associated: fever, rash, fatigue, weight loss?
- History: arthritis, gout, autoimmune conditions, recent injury?

## Fatigue / Tiredness

### Key Questions for Fatigue
- Duration: days, weeks, months?
- Severity: able to do daily activities? Bedridden?
- Sleep: quality, duration, snoring, frequently waking?
- Associated: weight changes, mood changes, appetite changes, fever?
- History: thyroid issues, anemia, diabetes, depression?
- Lifestyle: diet, exercise, stress level, work hours?

## Skin Problems

### Key Questions for Skin Issues
- Location: where on the body?
- Type: rash, itching, bump, discoloration, wound?
- Duration: when did it first appear?
- Changes: growing, spreading, changing color?
- Associated: fever, pain, swelling, discharge?
- Triggers: new products, foods, medications, sun exposure, contact with plants/animals?
- History: allergies, eczema, psoriasis, similar issues before?

## Breathing Difficulty

### Key Questions for Breathing Difficulty
- Onset: sudden or gradual?
- Severity: at rest or only with activity? How many words can you say in one breath?
- Duration: how long has this been happening?
- Position: worse lying down? Better sitting up?
- Associated: chest pain, cough, wheezing, fever, ankle swelling?
- History: asthma, COPD, heart disease, anxiety, smoking?
- Red flags: sudden severe shortness of breath, blue lips/fingers, inability to speak in full sentences

## Eye Problems

### Key Questions for Eye Issues
- Which eye: one or both?
- Symptoms: pain, redness, discharge, vision changes, itching, swelling?
- Duration: when did it start?
- Vision: blurry, double, loss of vision, floaters, flashes of light?
- Triggers: injury, contact lenses, allergies, screen time?
- Red flags: sudden vision loss, severe eye pain, flashes of light, chemical exposure

## Urinary Symptoms

### Key Questions for Urinary Issues
- Frequency: how often? More than usual?
- Pain/burning: during urination?
- Color: clear, dark, cloudy, blood?
- Associated: fever, back pain, abdominal pain?
- History: previous UTIs, kidney stones, prostate issues?

## Allergic Reactions

### Key Questions for Allergic Reactions
- Trigger: known allergen? Food, medication, insect sting, environmental?
- Symptoms: hives, swelling, itching, breathing difficulty?
- Severity: mild skin reaction or systemic?
- Duration: how quickly did symptoms appear?
- History: previous allergic reactions, EpiPen?
- Red flags: throat swelling, difficulty breathing, dizziness → ANAPHYLAXIS
