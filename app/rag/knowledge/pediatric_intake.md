# Pediatric Intake — Age-Specific Medical Questionnaire Guide

## General Principles for Pediatric Intake

### Parent/Guardian is the Reporter
- Questions should be directed at the parent/caregiver
- Use language like "Has your child..." instead of "Do you..."
- Ask about observed behaviors, not self-reported feelings
- Consider the child's age when selecting question options

### Age Group Considerations
- Infant (0-1 year): Focus on feeding, sleeping, crying patterns, developmental milestones
- Toddler (1-3 years): Behavior changes, eating, sleeping, activity level, milestones
- Preschool (3-6 years): Can express some symptoms verbally, observe behavior
- School-age (6-12 years): Can describe symptoms more clearly, ask age-appropriate questions
- Adolescent (12-18 years): Can self-report, but parent input valuable. Privacy considerations.

## Common Pediatric Presentations

### Fever in Children

#### Key Questions
- How high is the temperature? How was it measured? (oral, rectal, axillary, ear)
- How long has the fever lasted?
- How is the child's behavior? (playful/alert vs lethargic/irritable)
- Is the child eating and drinking normally?
- Any rash? Cough? Ear pulling? Vomiting? Diarrhea?
- Any sick contacts? (daycare, school, siblings)
- Up to date on vaccinations?
- Any febrile seizures in the past?

#### Red Flags in Pediatric Fever
- Infant < 3 months with fever > 38°C → urgent
- Child non-responsive or difficult to wake
- Rash that doesn't blanch (petechial/purpuric rash)
- Signs of dehydration (no tears, dry mouth, no urine 6+ hours)
- Neck stiffness + fever → possible meningitis

### Respiratory Symptoms in Children

#### Key Questions
- Cough: dry or wet? Barking (croup)? Wheezing?
- Breathing: fast breathing? Chest pulling in (retractions)? Nostril flaring?
- Feeding: difficulty feeding due to nasal congestion?
- Sleep: waking at night? Snoring? Pauses in breathing?
- History: asthma, allergies, premature birth?

#### Red Flags
- Blue lips or fingertips
- Severe chest retractions
- Unable to feed due to breathing difficulty
- Very fast breathing for age

### Gastrointestinal Symptoms in Children

#### Key Questions
- Vomiting: how many times? Projectile? Blood/bile?
- Diarrhea: how many episodes? Blood/mucus? Watery?
- Feeding: appetite changes? Refusal to eat?
- Hydration: how much fluid intake? Urine output?
- Abdominal pain: where? Can child localize it?

#### Red Flags
- Signs of dehydration (decreased urine, no tears, sunken eyes)
- Blood in vomit or stool
- Bilious (green) vomiting in infant
- Severe abdominal pain + guarding

### Skin Conditions in Children

#### Key Questions
- Where is the rash/lesion? Spreading?
- Itchy? Painful? Blistering?
- New exposures? (animals, plants, foods, laundry detergent)
- Fever with rash?
- Previous similar episodes? Known allergies? Eczema?

### Ear Problems in Children

#### Key Questions  
- Ear pulling or rubbing? (infants)
- Ear pain? Which ear?
- Drainage from ear?
- Fever? Irritability?
- Recent cold/upper respiratory infection?
- History of recurrent ear infections?

### Behavioral/Developmental Concerns

#### Key Questions
- Developmental milestones: on track for age?
- Sleep patterns: difficulty falling asleep, night waking, nightmares?
- Eating: picky eating, appetite changes, weight concerns?
- School performance: changes in grades, behavior at school?
- Social: playing with peers? Social withdrawal?
- Behavior: tantrums beyond typical age, aggression, hyperactivity?

## Tunisian Pediatric Expressions

### How Tunisian Parents Describe Their Child's Symptoms
- "ولدي/بنتي عندو/ها السخانة" = my son/daughter has fever
- "يبكي من غير سبب" = crying for no reason
- "ما يحبش ياكل" = doesn't want to eat
- "ما يرقدش بالليل" = doesn't sleep at night
- "عندو كحة وما يتنفسش مليح" = has a cough and can't breathe well
- "كرشو ماشية" = has diarrhea (stomach is running)
- "يتقيأ كل ما ياكل" = vomits everything they eat
- "حمرولو عينيه" = eyes are red
- "وذنو توجعو" = ear hurts
- "طلعتلو حبوب" = developed a rash/bumps
- "حسيتو ساخن" = I felt that they're hot (checking for fever by touch)

### Age-Specific Options for Questions
When asking about a child's symptoms, include age-appropriate options:
- Duration options: "Since today" / "Since yesterday" / "A few days" / "More than a week"
- Severity for children: "Playing normally" / "Less active than usual" / "Refusing to play" / "Very lethargic/sleepy"
- Feeding: "Eating/drinking normally" / "Less than usual" / "Refusing to eat/drink"
