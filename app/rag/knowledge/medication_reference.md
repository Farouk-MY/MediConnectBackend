# Medication Reference — Common Drug Categories for Medical Intake

## Pain Medications (Analgesics)

### Over-the-Counter (OTC)
- Paracetamol / Acetaminophen (Doliprane, Efferalgan, Tylenol) — fever and mild-moderate pain
- Ibuprofen (Advil, Nurofen, Brufen) — pain, inflammation, fever. NSAID.
- Aspirin (Aspro, Bayer) — pain, fever, blood thinning
- Diclofenac (Voltaren) — inflammatory pain, muscle pain. NSAID.

### Prescription
- Tramadol (Tramal, Contramal) — moderate-severe pain. Opioid.
- Codeine (with paracetamol: Codoliprane) — moderate pain. Opioid.
- Morphine and derivatives — severe pain. Strong opioid.

### Intake Question: "Are you taking any pain medication?"
Options should include: No medication / Paracetamol (Doliprane) / Ibuprofen (Advil, Brufen) / Prescribed painkillers / Other

## Antibiotics

### Common Categories
- Amoxicillin (Clamoxyl, Augmentin) — respiratory, urinary infections
- Azithromycin (Zithromax) — respiratory, ENT infections
- Ciprofloxacin (Ciflox) — urinary, GI infections
- Metronidazole (Flagyl) — anaerobic infections, GI

### Important Intake Questions About Antibiotics
- Currently on antibiotics? Which one? How many days?
- Completed the full course or stopped early?
- Any allergic reactions to antibiotics? (especially penicillin)

## Heart / Blood Pressure Medications

### Common in Tunisia/North Africa
- Amlodipine (Amlor) — calcium channel blocker for hypertension
- Losartan (Cozaar) — ARB for hypertension
- Atenolol / Bisoprolol — beta-blockers
- Enalapril / Ramipril — ACE inhibitors
- Hydrochlorothiazide — diuretic
- Aspirin low-dose (Kardegic 75mg, Aspégic) — blood thinning

### Intake Question
Options: No heart/BP medications / Blood pressure pills / Blood thinners / Cholesterol medication / Other

## Diabetes Medications

### Common
- Metformin (Glucophage, Stagid) — Type 2 diabetes, first-line
- Gliclazide (Diamicron) — sulfonylurea for Type 2
- Insulin (various types: rapid, long-acting)
- Glibenclamide (Daonil) — older sulfonylurea, still common in North Africa

### Intake Questions
- On insulin or tablets? Which ones?
- Do you monitor blood sugar at home?
- Any recent episodes of low blood sugar (hypoglycemia)?

## Respiratory Medications

### Common
- Salbutamol inhaler (Ventoline) — rescue bronchodilator
- Budesonide / Fluticasone (Pulmicort, Flixotide) — preventive inhaler (steroid)
- Montelukast (Singulair) — asthma prevention
- Antihistamines: Cetirizine (Zyrtec), Loratadine (Claritine) — allergies

## Digestive / GI Medications

### Common
- Omeprazole / Esomeprazole (Mopral, Inexium) — acid reflux, gastritis
- Domperidone (Motilium) — nausea, vomiting
- Loperamide (Imodium) — diarrhea
- Smecta (diosmectite) — very common in Tunisia for diarrhea
- Spasfon (phloroglucinol) — abdominal cramps, widely used in France/North Africa

## Mental Health Medications

### Common
- Paroxetine (Deroxat) — SSRI antidepressant
- Sertraline (Zoloft) — SSRI antidepressant
- Alprazolam (Xanax) — benzodiazepine, anxiety
- Bromazepam (Lexomil) — benzodiazepine, very common in Tunisia/France
- Escitalopram (Seroplex) — SSRI antidepressant

### Intake Sensitivity
- Ask about mental health medications gently
- Some patients may not want to disclose — offer "Prescribed medication" as a general option

## Supplements & Traditional Remedies (Common in Tunisia)

### Common supplements
- Vitamin D — very commonly prescribed in North Africa
- Iron supplements (Ferrograd, Tardyferon) — anemia
- Vitamin B12
- Folic acid — pregnancy
- Omega-3

### Traditional remedies patients may mention
- Black seed oil (حبة البركة / الحبة السوداء) — general health
- Olive oil (زيت الزيتون) — digestive health
- Honey + lemon — sore throat, cough
- Fenugreek (حلبة) — diabetes folk remedy
- Cumin (كمون) — digestive issues
- Harissa/spicy food — some believe it helps with colds

## Allergy Information

### Common Drug Allergies to Ask About
- Penicillin / Amoxicillin — most common drug allergy
- NSAIDs (Ibuprofen, Aspirin)
- Sulfonamides (sulfamethoxazole)
- Contrast dye (for imaging)

### Intake Question: "Do you have any drug allergies?"
Options: No known allergies / Penicillin / Aspirin/Ibuprofen / Other (specify)

## Tunisian/French Drug Names Patients May Use
- "Doliprane" = Paracetamol
- "Advil" or "Brufen" = Ibuprofen
- "Augmentin" = Amoxicillin + Clavulanic acid
- "Ventoline" = Salbutamol inhaler
- "la piqûre" = injection (patients may say "عملولي piqûre")
- "le sirop" = syrup medication
- "les cachets" = pills/tablets
- "les gouttes" = drops (eye, ear, nose)
- "la pommade" = ointment/cream
