# Chronic Conditions — Follow-up Patterns for Medical Intake

## Diabetes (Type 1 & Type 2)

### Key Intake Questions for Diabetic Patients
- Blood sugar control: Are you monitoring your blood sugar? Last HbA1c?
- Medications: Insulin, metformin, other oral medications? Dosage?
- Symptoms: excessive thirst, frequent urination, blurry vision, slow wound healing?
- Complications: numbness in feet (neuropathy), vision issues (retinopathy), kidney concerns?
- Diet: following a specific diet? Carbohydrate counting?
- Recent changes: medication adjustments, weight changes, new symptoms?
- Emergency signs: extreme thirst + confusion + fruity breath → possible DKA (diabetic ketoacidosis)

### When Patient Reports Symptoms + Has Diabetes
- Always ask about blood sugar levels
- Check if symptoms could be hyperglycemia or hypoglycemia
- Ask about recent medication changes
- Check foot health if presenting with any lower extremity complaint

## Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

### Key Intake Questions
- Monitoring: Do you check your blood pressure regularly? Last reading?
- Medications: Which BP medications? Taking them regularly?
- Symptoms: headaches, dizziness, chest pain, shortness of breath, vision changes?
- Lifestyle: salt intake, exercise, stress levels, smoking, alcohol?
- Complications: kidney problems, heart issues, stroke history?
- Emergency signs: severe headache + vision changes + chest pain → hypertensive crisis

### When Patient Has Known Hypertension
- Ask if they've been taking medications consistently
- Check for end-organ symptoms (vision, kidney, heart)
- Ask about lifestyle modifications

## Asthma / Respiratory Conditions

### Key Intake Questions
- Control: How often do you use your rescue inhaler?
- Triggers: allergens, exercise, cold air, smoke, dust, pets?
- Medications: preventive inhaler, oral medications, nebulizer?
- Severity: night symptoms, exercise limitations, hospitalizations?
- Recent changes: worsening, new triggers, medication changes?
- Action plan: Do you have an asthma action plan?
- Emergency signs: severe shortness of breath, cannot speak in sentences, blue lips

## Thyroid Disorders

### Key Intake Questions
- Type: hypothyroid or hyperthyroid? On medication?
- Symptoms hypothyroid: fatigue, weight gain, cold sensitivity, dry skin, constipation
- Symptoms hyperthyroid: weight loss, heat intolerance, rapid heartbeat, anxiety, tremor
- Medications: levothyroxine dosage? When do you take it?
- Last labs: recent thyroid function tests? TSH levels?

## Heart Disease / Cardiac Conditions

### Key Intake Questions
- Diagnosis: which heart condition? When diagnosed?
- Medications: blood thinners, beta-blockers, statins, aspirin?
- Symptoms: chest pain, shortness of breath, palpitations, ankle swelling, fatigue?
- Exercise tolerance: can you climb stairs? Walk a block?
- Recent changes: new symptoms, medication changes, hospitalizations?
- Emergency signs: chest pain at rest + sweating + shortness of breath

## Kidney Disease

### Key Intake Questions
- Stage: what stage of kidney disease?
- Dialysis: on dialysis? Type? Schedule?
- Medications: any restrictions?
- Symptoms: swelling (edema), fatigue, changes in urination, nausea?
- Diet: fluid restrictions, protein restrictions, potassium/phosphorus limits?

## Mental Health Conditions

### Key Intake Questions (approach gently)
- Condition: depression, anxiety, bipolar, other?
- Medications: antidepressants, anxiolytics, other psychotropics?
- Current state: how are you feeling lately? Sleep, appetite, energy?
- Support: seeing a therapist or counselor?
- Safety: any thoughts of self-harm? (ask if clinically appropriate)

## Autoimmune Conditions

### Key Intake Questions
- Diagnosis: which autoimmune condition? When diagnosed?
- Medications: immunosuppressants, biologics, steroids?
- Flare signs: increased symptoms, new joint involvement, rash changes?
- Infections: more frequent infections? Fever?
- Recent changes: medication adjustments, new symptoms?

## Cancer (Active or History)

### Key Intake Questions
- Type and stage: which cancer? Current treatment?
- Treatment: chemotherapy, radiation, surgery, immunotherapy?
- Side effects: from treatment — nausea, fatigue, pain, infections?
- Monitoring: recent scans, blood tests?
- Emergency signs: fever during chemo (neutropenic fever is life-threatening)
