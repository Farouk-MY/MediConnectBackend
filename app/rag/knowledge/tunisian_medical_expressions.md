# Tunisian Medical Expressions — Comprehensive Derja-to-Medical Dictionary

- وجه = face
- جبهة / الجبين = forehead
- عين / عينين = eye / eyes
- وذن / وذنين = ear / ears
- نيف / الأنف = nose
- فم / بوزل = mouth
- سنون / سنان = teeth
- حلق / الزور = throat
- رقبة / عنق = neck
- لسان = tongue
- شفار = lips

### Torso
- صدر = chest
- ظهر = back

- خصر = waist / lower back
- كتف / كتوفة = shoulder(s)
- قلب = heart
- رئة = lung
- كبد = liver

### Limbs
- ذراع / يد = arm / hand

## Symptom Descriptions — وصف الأعراض بالتونسي

### Pain Descriptions
- يوجعني = it hurts me
- يقطعني = sharp cutting pain
- يحرقني = it burns
- يكسرني = crushing/breaking pain
- يعصرني = squeezing pain
- ينخزني = stabbing pain
- يشدني = pulling/cramping pain
- يلسعني = stinging pain
- يخدرني = numbness/tingling
- تعبان/ة = sick/unwell/tired (general)

### Pain Intensity
- شوية = a little
- متاع حتى حاجة = nothing serious
- يضايقني = bothersome
- يوجعني برشة = hurts a lot
- ما نتحملوش = unbearable
- نموت من الوجع = dying from pain (expression for severe pain)
- وجع قوي / صعيب = strong/severe pain

### Time Expressions
- اليوم = today
- البارح = yesterday
- من كم يوم = a few days ago
- هالجمعة = this week
- من شهر = a month ago
- من زمان = a long time ago
- ديما = always / constantly
- ساعات = sometimes
- كل يوم = every day
- بالليل = at night
- بالنهار = during the day
- الصباح = in the morning
- كي نوقف = when I stand up
- كي نمشي = when I walk
- كي نرقد = when I lie down

### Frequency
- مرة وحدة = once
- كل يوم = every day
- كل ساعة = every hour
- ساعات ساعات = from time to time
- نهار إيه نهار لا = some days yes, some days no
- كل ما ناكل = every time I eat

## Common Medical Situations — بالتونسي

### At the Doctor
- نحب نتعالج = I want treatment
- نحب نتقابل مع الطبيب = I want to see the doctor
- عطاني الطبيب دواء = the doctor gave me medicine
- عملت تحليل = I had a test/analysis done
- عملت راديو / الريو = I had an X-ray (radio)
- عملت سكانار = I had a CT scan
- عملت إيكو = I had an ultrasound (échographie)
- نحب نعمل موعد = I want to make an appointment
- هل نخلص = do I pay?

### Medication-Related
- نشرب دواء = I take medicine (in Tunisia "نشرب" means take, not drink)
- الكاشيات = pills/tablets (from French "cachets")
- السيرو = syrup (from French "sirop")
- البيقورة = injection (from French "piqûre")
- القطرات = drops
- البوماد = ointment/cream (from French "pommade")
- الحبوب = pills
- نلصق لبلاستر = I put on a bandage/plaster
- ما نشربش الدواء = I don't take the medicine (non-compliance)

### Emergency Expressions
- نحس باش نطيح = I feel like I'm going to faint
- ما نجمش نتنفس = I can't breathe
- عندي نزيف = I'm bleeding
- بالسيف نمشي لسبيطار = I need to go to the hospital urgently
- نحس روحي باش نموت = I feel like I'm dying (extreme distress)
- عييت = I'm exhausted / I've had enough

## French-Arabic Mixed Expressions (Code-Switching)

### Very Common in Tunisian Medical Context
- "عندي دولور في..." = I have pain in... (douleur)
- "عندي لا فياڤر" = I have fever (la fièvre)
- "عندي لا ناوزي" = I have nausea (la nausée)
- "عملولي أوردونونس" = they gave me a prescription (ordonnance)
- "لازمني أوبيراسيون" = I need surgery (opération)
- "مشيت للأورجونس" = I went to the ER (urgences)
- "عندي لا بريسيون" = I have blood pressure issues (la pression)
- "عندي لو ديابيت" = I have diabetes (le diabète)
- "عندي لاسم" = I have asthma (l'asthme)
- "لالارجي" = allergy (l'allergie)
- "عندي لو رووم" = I have a cold (le rhume)
- "نورمال" = normal
- "ڤراف" = serious/grave (grave)
- "إكزامان" = medical exam (examen)
- "أناليز" = lab tests (analyses)

## Cultural Context for AI

### How Tunisian Patients Express Themselves
1. They often understate symptoms initially — probe gently
2. Pain might be described metaphorically: "my head is on fire" = severe headache
3. "بالصح" (but/however) often precedes the real concern — pay attention to what follows
4. "عادي" (normal) might mean they're minimizing — ask follow-up
5. Family members often speak on behalf of the patient, especially for elderly
6. "سبيطار" = hospital (from French "hôpital" → Italian "ospedale")
7. Patients may use "نحس" (I feel) for everything — probe for specifics
8. "ما نرقدش" = I can't sleep — very common complaint, often linked to pain or anxiety
