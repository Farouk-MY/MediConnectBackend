# Medical Intake Question Templates by Body System

## General / Constitutional
- How long have you been feeling this way?
- Have you noticed any fever or chills?
- Any unintentional weight loss or gain recently?
- How is your appetite?
- How is your energy level — do you feel more tired than usual?
- Have you had any night sweats?

## Head / Neurological
- Where exactly in your head do you feel the pain?
- Is the headache on one side or both sides?
- Do you see any visual changes (spots, blurriness, light sensitivity)?
- Any dizziness, lightheadedness, or feeling faint?
- Any numbness or tingling in your face, arms, or legs?
- Any difficulty with speech or confusion?
- Have you had any seizures or loss of consciousness?

## Eyes
- When did your eye problem start?
- Which eye is affected, or both?
- Any pain, redness, or discharge?
- Any changes in vision (blurry, double, loss of vision)?
- Any sensitivity to light?

## Ears / Nose / Throat
- Any ear pain or hearing changes?
- Any ringing in the ears (tinnitus)?
- Any nasal congestion, runny nose, or sinus pressure?
- Any sore throat or difficulty swallowing?
- Any hoarseness or voice changes?
- Any swollen glands or lumps in the neck?

## Respiratory / Chest
- Are you having any difficulty breathing?
- Do you have a cough? Is it dry or does it produce mucus?
- What color is the mucus, if any?
- Any wheezing or chest tightness?
- Does breathing worsen with activity or lying down?
- Any chest pain? Where exactly?

## Cardiovascular
- Do you have chest pain or pressure?
- Does the pain spread to your arm, jaw, or back?
- Any palpitations (feeling your heart racing or skipping)?
- Any swelling in your legs or ankles?
- Do you get short of breath when climbing stairs or walking?
- Any episodes of fainting or near-fainting?

## Gastrointestinal / Abdominal
- Where exactly in your abdomen do you feel the pain?
- Any nausea or vomiting?
- Any changes in bowel habits (diarrhea, constipation)?
- Any blood in your stool or black/tarry stools?
- Any heartburn or acid reflux?
- Any bloating or gas?
- When did you last eat? Does eating affect the symptom?

## Musculoskeletal
- Which joint or body part is affected?
- Is there any swelling, redness, or warmth?
- Any stiffness? When is it worst (morning, after rest, after activity)?
- Did you have any injury or fall recently?
- Does the pain limit your movement?
- Have you tried any pain relief?

## Skin / Dermatological
- When did the skin issue first appear?
- Where on your body is it located?
- Has it spread or changed since it started?
- Any itching, burning, or pain?
- Any new products, detergents, or exposures?
- Any similar episodes in the past?

## Urological / Renal
- Any pain or burning during urination?
- Any change in urine color (dark, bloody, cloudy)?
- Any increased frequency or urgency to urinate?
- Any difficulty starting or stopping urination?
- Any lower back or flank pain?

## Psychiatric / Mental Health
- How has your mood been recently?
- Any significant changes in sleep patterns?
- Any feelings of anxiety or excessive worry?
- Any loss of interest in activities you normally enjoy?
- Any difficulty concentrating?
- (SENSITIVE - handled with care): Any thoughts of harming yourself or others?

## Pediatric Additions (when patient is a child)
- Has your child's behavior changed recently?
- Any changes in eating or drinking habits?
- Is your child more irritable or clingy than usual?
- Any new rashes or spots?
- How is your child sleeping?

## Obstetric/Gynecological
- When was your last menstrual period?
- Any unusual bleeding or discharge?
- Any possibility of pregnancy?
- Any lower abdominal/pelvic pain?

## Follow-up Pattern Questions
After identifying the main complaint area, the system should follow this pattern:
1. Clarify location and character
2. Establish timeline (onset, duration, frequency)
3. Assess severity (1-10 scale, impact on daily life)
4. Identify associated symptoms
5. Ask about aggravating/alleviating factors
6. Check relevant medical history and medications
7. Assess for red flag symptoms
8. Summarize and confirm understanding
