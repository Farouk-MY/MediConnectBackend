# Symptom Assessment — Medical History Taking Best Practices

## The HPI Framework (History of Present Illness)

When a patient presents with a symptom, the clinical standard for gathering information follows the **OLDCARTS** mnemonic:

### O — Onset
- When did the symptom first appear?
- Was the onset sudden or gradual?
- What were you doing when it started?

### L — Location
- Where exactly do you feel the symptom?
- Does it stay in one place or move/radiate?
- Can you point to the area?

### D — Duration
- How long does each episode last?
- Is it constant or does it come and go?
- How long have you had this problem overall?

### C — Character/Quality
- What does the symptom feel like?
- For pain: sharp, dull, burning, throbbing, stabbing, aching, cramping, pressure?
- For other symptoms: describe the sensation in your own words

### A — Aggravating/Alleviating Factors
- What makes it worse? (movement, eating, stress, position, time of day)
- What makes it better? (rest, medication, heat/cold, position)
- Have you tried any treatment?

### R — Radiation
- Does the sensation spread to other body areas?
- For pain: does it travel to your arm, back, leg, jaw?

### T — Timing
- When does it happen? (morning, night, after meals, during activity)
- How often does it occur?
- Is there a pattern?

### S — Severity
- On a scale of 1-10, how severe is it?
- Does it interfere with daily activities?
- Does it wake you from sleep?

## Additional Important Intake Questions

### Associated Symptoms
- Do you have any other symptoms occurring at the same time?
- Fever, nausea, vomiting, changes in appetite, weight changes?
- Any recent changes in sleep, mood, or energy levels?

### Past Medical History
- Have you experienced this before?
- Do you have any chronic medical conditions?
- Have you had any recent surgeries or hospitalizations?

### Medications
- Are you currently taking any medications?
- Over-the-counter medications, supplements, or herbal remedies?
- Have you recently changed any medications?

### Allergies
- Do you have any known drug allergies?
- Do you have any food or environmental allergies?

### Social/Environmental Factors
- Any recent travel?
- Any recent changes in diet, lifestyle, or stress levels?
- Exposure to anyone who is ill?

## Age-Appropriate Considerations
- For elderly patients: use simpler language, allow more time
- For parents reporting for children: ask about behavior changes, eating patterns
- Cultural sensitivity: respect patient's comfort level with discussing certain topics

## Tunisian/North African Cultural Considerations
- Many patients prefer describing symptoms in Tunisian Arabic (Derja)
- Pain descriptions may be metaphorical: "my head is on fire" (راسي يحرق)
- Patients may reference traditional remedies or practices
- Be respectful of all approaches while gathering clinical information
- Some patients may be hesitant about certain topics — provide gentle encouragement
