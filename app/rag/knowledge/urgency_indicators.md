# Urgency Indicators and Red Flag Symptoms

## Critical (Immediately Life-Threatening)

### Cardiovascular Emergency
- Severe crushing chest pain, especially radiating to left arm, jaw, or back
- Chest pain with profuse sweating, nausea, and shortness of breath
- Sudden severe difficulty breathing with chest tightness
- Signs of cardiac arrest: unresponsive, not breathing normally

### Neurological Emergency
- Sudden onset of facial drooping, arm weakness, or speech difficulty (FAST signs)
- "Worst headache of my life" — sudden, thunderclap onset
- Sudden loss of vision in one or both eyes
- Sudden severe confusion or inability to understand speech
- Seizures lasting more than 5 minutes or multiple seizures without recovery

### Respiratory Emergency
- Severe difficulty breathing, unable to complete sentences
- Choking with inability to cough, speak, or breathe
- Blue lips or fingertips (cyanosis)
- Severe asthma attack not responding to usual inhaler

### Hemorrhagic Emergency
- Severe bleeding that won't stop with pressure
- Vomiting large amounts of blood
- Coughing up blood in significant quantities
- Severe rectal bleeding with dizziness

### Allergic Emergency
- Anaphylaxis: throat swelling, difficulty breathing, rapid heartbeat
- Severe allergic reaction with hives, swelling, and breathing difficulty
- Known severe allergy exposure with escalating symptoms

### Psychiatric Emergency
- Active suicidal ideation with plan or means
- Self-harm in progress
- Severe psychotic episode with danger to self or others

### Obstetric Emergency
- Severe vaginal bleeding during pregnancy
- Severe abdominal pain during pregnancy with bleeding
- Eclamptic seizures

## High (Urgent — Needs Prompt Medical Attention)

### Cardiovascular
- Persistent chest pain lasting more than 20 minutes
- Severe palpitations with dizziness or shortness of breath
- Sudden severe leg pain and swelling (possible deep vein thrombosis)
- Fainting episodes (syncope) without clear cause

### Neurological
- Sudden persistent weakness or numbness in any limb
- Severe persistent headache with fever and neck stiffness (meningitis signs)
- New onset seizure
- Sudden significant vision changes
- Persistent severe dizziness or vertigo with vomiting

### Abdominal
- Severe abdominal pain with rigid abdomen
- Persistent vomiting unable to keep fluids down for 24+ hours
- Severe abdominal pain with high fever
- Signs of intestinal obstruction (severe pain, no bowel movements, distension)

### Infection
- High fever (>39.5°C/103°F) with confusion or lethargy
- Signs of sepsis: fever, rapid heart rate, rapid breathing, confusion
- Severe pain, swelling, redness spreading rapidly (cellulitis)

### Trauma
- Significant injury with deformity (possible fracture)
- Head injury with confusion, vomiting, or loss of consciousness
- Eye injury with vision changes

## Medium (Should Be Seen Soon)

- Persistent fever > 38.5°C for more than 3 days
- Moderate chest pain that comes and goes
- Significant shortness of breath with exertion
- Persistent severe headache unresponsive to OTC medication
- Urinary symptoms with fever and back pain
- Significant unintentional weight loss
- Persistent unexplained fatigue
- New lumps or masses
- Persistent change in bowel habits
- Depression with social withdrawal

## Low (Routine — Normal Consultation Appropriate)

- Mild to moderate headache
- Common cold symptoms (runny nose, mild cough, mild sore throat)
- Mild muscle or joint pain
- Minor skin rashes or irritation
- Mild digestive complaints
- Mild urinary symptoms without fever
- Routine follow-up for chronic conditions
- Preventive care questions
- Mild anxiety or stress-related concerns

## Safe Escalation Language

When flagging urgent cases, the system must:
1. NOT panic the patient
2. NOT diagnose
3. Provide a gentle recommendation
4. Use the patient's language

### Examples by language:

**English (High):**
"Your symptoms suggest you should seek medical attention soon. Please consider visiting your nearest urgent care center or emergency room. If your symptoms worsen suddenly, please call emergency services (SAMU: 190)."

**French (High):**
"Vos symptômes suggèrent que vous devriez consulter un médecin rapidement. Nous vous recommandons de vous rendre aux urgences les plus proches. En cas d'aggravation soudaine, appelez le SAMU (190)."

**Arabic (High):**
"أعراضك تشير إلى أنه يجب عليك مراجعة طبيب بشكل عاجل. يرجى التوجه إلى أقرب قسم طوارئ. في حالة تفاقم الأعراض فجأة، اتصل بالإسعاف (190)."

**Tunisian Arabic (High):**
"الأعراض متاعك تدل إنك لازم تمشي تشوف طبيب بالسيف. روح لأقرب سبيطار ولا طوارئ. كان الأعراض زادت وخيانتك، عيط للإسعاف (190)."
