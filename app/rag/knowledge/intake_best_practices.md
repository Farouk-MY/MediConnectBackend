# Medical Intake Best Practices

## Core Principles

### 1. Patient-Centered Communication
- Use open-ended questions to start, then narrow down with specific choices
- Match language complexity to the patient's literacy level
- Show empathy and patience — patients may be anxious or in pain
- Avoid medical jargon unless the patient uses it first
- Confirm understanding by summarizing back

### 2. One Question at a Time
- Never bundle multiple questions into one
- Wait for a complete answer before moving on
- Each question should have a single clear purpose
- If the patient gives information about multiple topics, acknowledge it and follow up on each separately

### 3. Progressive Narrowing
Good intake follows a funnel pattern:
1. **Open**: "What brings you in today?" → Free text
2. **Clarifying**: "Where exactly do you feel the pain?" → Free text or body area selection
3. **Specific**: "How would you describe the pain?" → Multiple choice (sharp, dull, burning, etc.)
4. **Quantifying**: "On a scale of 1-10, how severe is it?" → Scale selection
5. **Temporal**: "How long has this been going on?" → Duration selection
6. **Associated**: "Do you have any other symptoms?" → Checkbox list
7. **History**: "Have you experienced this before?" → Yes/No with follow-up
8. **Medications**: "Are you taking any medications?" → Yes/No with follow-up

### 4. Answer Format Selection
Choose the right input type for each question:

| Scenario | Best Format | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Single clear answer expected | Radio buttons | "Where is the pain?" → Head / Chest / Abdomen / Back / Limbs / Other |
| Multiple answers possible | Checkbox chips | "Which symptoms do you have?" → Nausea / Fever / Headache / Fatigue / Other |
| Severity/intensity | Radio scale | "Pain level?" → Mild / Moderate / Severe / Very severe |
| Timeline/duration | Radio options | "How long?" → Today / Few days / 1-2 weeks / More than a month |
| Detailed description needed | Free text | "Describe the sensation" |
| Yes/No with elaboration | Radio + conditional text | "Taking medications?" → Yes (specify) / No |

### 5. Avoid Leading Questions
- ❌ "The pain is probably sharp, right?"
- ✅ "How would you describe the pain?"
- ❌ "You don't have any serious conditions, do you?"
- ✅ "Do you have any existing medical conditions?"

### 6. Handle Sensitive Topics
- Mental health: approach gently, normalize the question
- Reproductive health: be respectful, use clinical terms
- Substance use: frame as medical necessity without judgment
- Domestic violence: never ask unless trained to handle response

## Cultural Sensitivity for North African Patients

### Language Considerations
- Tunisian patients often speak Derja (تونسي), not Modern Standard Arabic
- Common Tunisian medical expressions:
  - "عندي وجع" (I have pain) — general
  - "راسي يدور" (my head is spinning) — dizziness
  - "كرشي توجعني" (my stomach hurts)
  - "ما نجمش نتنفس" (I can't breathe)
  - "عندي السخانة" (I have fever)
  - "جسمي يحرق" (my body is burning) — fever or pain
  - "نحس روحي تعبان/ة" (I feel tired/unwell)
- Many patients mix French and Arabic: "عندي douleur في le dos"
- Respect this natural code-switching — don't force one language

### Cultural Norms
- Older patients may prefer formal address
- Family involvement in health decisions is common and expected
- Some patients may attribute symptoms to spiritual causes — acknowledge respectfully
- Pain expression varies by culture — some underreport, some express more vocally
- Food and dietary habits are important context (Ramadan fasting, traditional diet)

## Data Quality Guidelines

### What Makes a Good Intake Summary
1. **Complete**: Covers all relevant OLDCARTS dimensions
2. **Accurate**: Reflects exactly what the patient said, not interpretation
3. **Organized**: Structured for quick doctor review
4. **Actionable**: Highlights what the doctor needs to explore further
5. **Safe**: Flags any urgent symptoms clearly

### Information Prioritization
Rank information by clinical utility:
1. 🔴 Chief complaint and urgency indicators (CRITICAL)
2. 🟠 Symptom details: location, severity, duration (HIGH)
3. 🟡 Associated symptoms and triggers (MEDIUM)
4. 🟢 Medical history and medications (IMPORTANT)
5. 🔵 Lifestyle and social factors (HELPFUL)

### When to End the Questionnaire Early
End questionnaire if:
- Urgent symptoms are detected — recommend immediate care
- All key dimensions of the chief complaint are covered
- Patient becomes frustrated or fatigued
- Patient provides enough detail for a useful summary
- Maximum question count is reached

### When to Continue
Continue if:
- Key dimensions (duration, severity, location) are still unknown
- Patient has mentioned concerning symptoms that need clarification
- Associated symptoms suggest a different body system involvement
- Medication or allergy information is relevant but not yet collected
